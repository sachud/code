### **Categories by Methodology (Knowledge Level)**

- **Black Box Testing:** The hacker has no prior information about the system, mimicking a real-world external attacker.

- **White Box Testing:** The hacker has full knowledge, including source code and network diagrams, allowing for a deep internal audit.

- **Grey Box Testing:** A hybrid approach where the hacker has partial information, such as low-level user credentials

### **Categories by Intention ("Goals")**

- **White Hat:** Legal professionals (ethical hackers) hired to find and fix security flaws.

- **Black Hat:** Malicious actors who break into systems illegally for personal gain or harm.

- **Grey Hat:** Hackers who may find vulnerabilities without permission but report them to the owner, often for a fee, sitting in a legal grey area.

- **Red Hat:** "Vigilante" hackers who actively target and dismantle black hat infrastructure to stop them. 

### **Categories by Operational Role**

- **Red Teaming:** Simulating advanced, stealthy attacks to test a company’s overall detection and response capabilities.
- **Blue Teaming:** The defensive side, focusing on continuous monitoring, incident response, and hardening systems against the red team.
- **Purple Teaming:** Collaborative exercises where red and blue teams work together to improve security through immediate feedback.

Red Team → Attackers (ethical hackers)
Blue Team → Defenders (SOC, incident response)
Purple Team → Collaboration of both

### **Phases of a Pentest**

- **Reconnaissance:** Gathering intel on the target using tools like Nmap or Shodan.
- **Scanning:** Actively probing for open ports and services.
- **Vulnerability Assessment:** Analyzing discovered data to identify potential entry points.
- **Exploitation:** Safely attempting to breach the system to prove the risk is real.
- **Reporting:** Documenting findings with prioritized remediation steps for developers and stakeholders.
